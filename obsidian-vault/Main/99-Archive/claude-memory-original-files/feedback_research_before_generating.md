When generating AI art (for Etsy, Skool challenges, or any design work), NEVER invent prompts from imagination. Always research first.

**Why:** On 2026-03-28, when asked to create a competition-winning design, I wrote 150+ word flowery prompts from imagination ("luminous", "ethereal", "bioluminescent"). The results looked like digital illustrations, not oil paintings. Roberto called it out: "None of them look like an oil painting." The proven Midjourney oil painting prompts in NocoDB are 20-50 words and follow a specific formula.

**How to apply:**
1. Use the `art-prompt-builder` skill (at `/home/mello/skills/art-prompt-builder/`)
2. Pull reference prompts from NocoDB Skool Prompts table (`m3082prazskg7qd`)
3. Analyze the pattern (length, structure, keywords, painter refs)
4. Build the new prompt following that exact formula
5. Show Roberto the references AND the built prompt before generating
