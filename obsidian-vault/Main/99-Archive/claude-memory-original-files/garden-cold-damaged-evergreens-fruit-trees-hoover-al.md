Garden data lives in the **mello-garden** app (https://garden.synai.ai, port 3010, DB `mello_garden` on n8n-postgres) and is surfaced through the **garden-telegram** bot (reads GET /api/plants; new plants appear automatically). Location: **Hoover, Alabama, zone 8a**.

**Four in-ground evergreens** recovering from the **Dec 2022 Arctic freeze (~7F)**, all acid-loving, all fed **Espoma Holly-Tone (4-3-4)**: American Holly (Ilex opaca, hardy to ~-10F), Chinese Fringe Flower/Loropetalum (~5F), Fragrant Tea Olive (Osmanthus fragrans, TENDEREST ~10F, protect every winter), Indian Hawthorns. Mature 10-15yr trees, died back from the top and resprouting from the base. Recovery care: light Holly-Tone early March + late spring, stop by late summer; deep water (~1in/wk); 2-3in pine-straw mulch kept off the trunk; never pile soil over the root zone. **Wisteria (invasive) encroaches around all of them** and must be cleared from the root zones.

**Container tropical fruit trees** (Avocado, Goiaba/Guava, Lime) nearly died last winter and are regrowing; NOT cold hardy (minTemp 35-40F), need winter protection/garage.

**Fig tree**: pruned to 5-6 keeper stems (worked very well, vigorous leafy regrowth). Plan to move from container to the ground for bigger growth + more fruit. Best timing zone 8a: late winter while dormant (Feb-early March) is safest; fall after leaf-drop also works with heavy mulch + first-winter protection. Peach tree similarly slated to move to ground (fall 2026).
